# Paloma365 Kitchen Display System — FAQ

## What is the Kitchen Display System?

KDS replaces paper tickets with digital screens in the kitchen. Orders are
routed per station (grill, cold, dessert), each dish carries a preparation
timer, and overdue tickets flash for the expeditor. Cook-time analytics by
dish and daypart come out of the box.

## How does KDS reduce kitchen time?

Three mechanisms. First, station routing removes ticket hand-offs: each
station sees only its own items. Second, target timers make slow dishes
visible in real time instead of at the end-of-shift report. Third,
cook-time analytics show which dishes and dayparts break the target, so
the chef can fix recipes or staffing precisely. Venues with 20+ minute
average kitchen times typically return to their target within one to two
months of adoption.

## What hardware is required?

One wall-mounted display per kitchen station (any HDMI screen works) and
one Android tablet for the expeditor. Screens are not included in the
module price. A typical two-station kitchen needs about 150,000 KZT of
one-time hardware on top of the module setup fee.

## Does KDS work during internet outages?

Yes. The kitchen queue is served from the local Paloma365 terminal; orders
keep flowing between POS and kitchen screens over the local network.
Cloud analytics resume syncing when connectivity returns.
